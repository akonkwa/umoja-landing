# Winko Draft Updated Proposal and Commercial Summary

## Winko: Updated proposal and technical response - Edward Francis Small Teaching Hospital (EFSTH), Banjul, The Gambia

## 1. Executive Summary

Winko proposes an updated solar PV + battery energy system for Edward Francis Small Teaching Hospital (EFSTH) to support continuous power for the oxygen production system and other agreed critical loads.

This revised draft updates the previously proposed configuration by:

- increasing solar PV capacity from `140 kWp` to `170 kWp`
- increasing usable battery storage from `522 kWh` to `622 kWh`
- maintaining the hybrid inverter platform at `125 kW`, which remains adequate for the validated oxygen-plant load profile and the revised PV DC/AC ratio
- adding the automated PV cleaning system into the total project price
- incorporating the Bill of Materials (`BoM`) into the contract as a formal appendix

The design basis remains the same as validated on site on `January 19, 2026`:

- Base load: `15 kW`
- Average load: `25 kW`
- Peak load: `35 kW` cyclic compressor demand
- Daily energy demand: `600 kWh/day`

### Updated indicative system sizing

| Item | Previous Basis | Revised Basis |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| Solar PV capacity | 140 kWp | 170 kWp |
| Approximate PV modules | ~300 modules | ~365 modules |
| Usable battery capacity | 522 kWh | 622 kWh |
| Hybrid inverter capacity | 125 kW | 125 kW |

The additional `30 kWp` of PV improves daytime production margin and battery charging headroom. The additional `100 kWh` of usable storage increases outage-bridging capability and improves resilience during late-day or overnight grid interruptions. The cleaning system improves actual delivered PV output by reducing Harmattan dust, salt spray residue, and biological soiling losses.

## 2. Technical Approach

### 2.1 Design basis

The revised system continues to use a distributed rooftop architecture across the suitable EFSTH buildings, with battery and inverter equipment located as close as practical to the oxygen plant interface in order to reduce cable runs, simplify protection, and improve response during outages.

The architecture remains:

- distributed rooftop PV arrays
- LiFePO4 battery storage
- hybrid inverter system with controlled solar/battery/grid operation
- dedicated oxygen-plant circuits with clear labeling and isolation
- remote monitoring with alarms, event logs, and reporting

### 2.2 Cleaning system integration

The automated cleaning scope is incorporated as an ad hoc addition to the main EPC package and revised to match the larger PV field of approximately `365 modules`.

The cleaning concept remains:

- `Zone A`: rail-based motorized cleaning on the main uniform roof sections
- `Zones B and C`: tethered robotic cleaning for distributed and irregular roof sections
- shared water infrastructure, monitoring, cleaning-cycle logging, and fault alerts

This combined approach remains appropriate for EFSTH because the roofs are distributed across several buildings and one cleaning technology alone would not cover the whole site efficiently.
